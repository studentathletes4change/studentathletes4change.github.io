How to Combat Racism after the Hashtags Stop Trending

Katie Berkowitz UMBC Women's Soccer '19

Supporting the Black Lives Matter movement is not a political issue. It is a moral one.

If you hear someone state “Black Lives Matter” and your immediate response is to try to refute that, please ask yourselves why. Because the only reason to argue that statement is if you believe that Black lives don’t matter.

If you cannot see that Black people are treated exponentially worse than white people by essentially every American institution, you have purposefully chosen to turn a blind eye or chosen to be willfully ignorant. If you know it to be true that Black people (as well as other racial and ethnic minorities) are mistreated and choose to ignore it, or stay silent, you are exercising your white privilege, which is inexcusable.

“Neutrality helps the oppressor, never the victim.” It is not acceptable to stay neutral. We must fight for Black lives. Attached is a link as to what you personally can do to help the movement and demand justice for George Floyd, Breonna Taylor, Ahmaud Arbery, Tony McDade and others.

https://blacklivesmatters.carrd.co/

This fight cannot stop when the hashtag stops trending. We must commit to fight racism and racist institutions. That begins with educating ourselves on what it means to be anti racist, here are several books that are a good start.

Me and White Supremacy by Layla F. Saad
How to Be an Antiracist by Ibram X. Kendi
Between the World and Me by Ta-Nehisi Coates
Why Are All the Black Kids Sitting Together in the Cafeteria? by Beverly Daniel Tatum

Lastly, many of the people who have turned to quotes by MLK Jr. to argue against violent protests are forgetting what he said regarding riots: "A riot is the language of the unheard." Before you criticize how people are protesting, ask yourself why the destruction of a country that Black people suffered to build is so much more upsetting to you than the murder of an innocent Black man. Property is never worth more than human life.

If you are still unconvinced that Black lives are worth fighting for, I am both saddened and horrified. Black people should not have to justify their humanity to society.
